# 📊 Mock Test Review Workflow

> **Turn every mock test into a precise improvement plan.**

Most students look at their score, feel bad, and move on. This workflow turns every mock into 3× the learning value.

**Time required:** 2-3 hours after each mock  
**When:** Within 24 hours of the test

---

## Why This Workflow Matters

> Your mock test score tells you *what* happened. AI analysis tells you *why* and *what to do next*.

---

## The 4-Phase Mock Review

### Phase 1: Categorize Every Wrong Answer (30 min)

First, go through every question you got wrong. Categorize each:

- **Type A:** "I didn't know the concept" — knowledge gap
- **Type B:** "I knew it but made a silly mistake" — carelessness
- **Type C:** "I was confused between 2 options" — weak understanding
- **Type D:** "I didn't have time" — time management
- **Type E:** "I guessed wrong" — acceptable

Then run this prompt:

```
I just completed a [JEE Main/NEET/CBSE] mock test.
My results:
- Total score: [X/Y]
- Physics: [score] | Mistakes: [list topic of each wrong Q]
- Chemistry: [score] | Mistakes: [list topic of each wrong Q]  
- Maths/Biology: [score] | Mistakes: [list topic of each wrong Q]

Error breakdown:
- Knowledge gaps (Type A): [topics]
- Silly mistakes (Type B): [count]
- Conceptual confusion (Type C): [topics]
- Time issues (Type D): [sections]

Please:
1. Identify my top 3 weak areas by category
2. Tell me if any pattern exists (e.g., "You keep making errors in electrostatics application")
3. Prioritize what to fix first for maximum score improvement
4. Build a 3-day recovery plan
```

---

### Phase 2: Deep-Dive on Type A Errors (60 min)

For every knowledge gap topic:

```
I got questions on [TOPIC] wrong in my mock test because I didn't know the concept.

The question(s) I got wrong:
[PASTE QUESTION IF YOU HAVE IT]

Please:
1. Explain the concept from the beginning
2. Show me how to solve this type of question
3. Give me 3 similar questions with solutions
4. Tell me what to memorize to never get this wrong again
```

---

### Phase 3: Fix Type B (Silly Mistakes) (20 min)

```
I made [X] silly mistakes in today's mock despite knowing the concepts.

My mistakes were: [describe 2-3 examples]

Help me:
1. Identify if there's a pattern to my silly mistakes
2. Give me a "before submitting" mental checklist
3. Suggest 2-3 habits to reduce careless errors
4. Tell me how top JEE/NEET scorers approach this
```

---

### Phase 4: Build the Recovery Plan (20 min)

```
Based on my mock test analysis:
- Weak areas: [from Phase 1]
- Days until next mock/exam: [X]

Create a day-by-day study plan:
- What to study each day
- Which topics to prioritize vs maintain
- How many practice questions per topic
- When to take the next mock
Format: Day 1, Day 2, Day 3... table format.
```

---

## 📋 Mock Review Checklist

- [ ] Categorized every wrong answer (A/B/C/D/E)
- [ ] Ran Phase 1 analysis prompt
- [ ] Fixed all Type A knowledge gaps
- [ ] Addressed silly mistake patterns
- [ ] Created recovery plan
- [ ] Scheduled next mock test

## 📈 Track Your Progress

Keep a mock test log:

| Date | Mock | Score | Top 3 Weak Areas | Fixed? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [date] | Mock 1 | [score] | [topics] | [ ] |

After 5 mocks, run:
```
Here are my last 5 mock test scores and weak areas: [paste your log]
Show me: am I improving? What's my persistent weakness?
Build a 2-week intensive plan to address it.
```
