# 📖 New Chapter Workflow

> **How to study any new chapter using AI — from first read to exam-ready.**

**Time required:** 2-4 hours  
**Works for:** JEE, NEET, CBSE any class  
**Best AI tool:** Claude (free tier works)

---

## The 5-Step Chapter Workflow

### ✅ Step 1: Reconnaissance (10 min)

Before reading a single page, run this prompt:

```
I'm about to start [CHAPTER NAME] in [SUBJECT] for [EXAM].
I haven't studied it yet.

Tell me:
1. What is this chapter fundamentally about? (3 sentences)
2. What should I already know before starting?
3. What are the 3 most important concepts in this chapter?
4. How is this chapter tested in [EXAM]? (question types, weightage)
5. What's the #1 mistake students make with this chapter?
```

**Why:** Knowing where you're going makes reading 3× faster.

---

### ✅ Step 2: Concept-by-Concept Study (60-90 min)

Read one section of your textbook/notes. Then for each concept:

```
I just read about [CONCEPT] in [CHAPTER].
Here's what I think I understood: [YOUR EXPLANATION IN 2-3 SENTENCES]

Check my understanding:
1. Am I right? What did I miss or misunderstand?
2. Clarify the part most students get wrong
3. Give me one worked example with full solution
4. One question to test if I've really got it
```

**Loop this for every major concept in the chapter.**

---

### ✅ Step 3: Connect the Chapter (20 min)

After covering all concepts:

```
I've now studied all of [CHAPTER]. Help me see the full picture:

1. How do all the concepts in this chapter relate to each other?
2. What's the logical flow from start to finish?
3. Which concepts depend on which?
4. How does this chapter connect to [PREVIOUS CHAPTERS]?
5. Create a 10-point summary of the entire chapter.
```

---

### ✅ Step 4: Exam-Focused Preparation (30 min)

```
I've finished studying [CHAPTER] for [EXAM].
Now help me prepare specifically for the exam:

1. List the 5 most likely question types from this chapter
2. Show me 2 examples of each type (with solutions)
3. What shortcuts or tricks apply?
4. Create a "cheat sheet" — what I'd write on a flashcard
5. Common traps: what wrong answers look like and why they're wrong
```

---

### ✅ Step 5: Self-Test (20-30 min)

The most important step. Don't skip it.

```
Quiz me on [CHAPTER] with 12 questions:
- 4 easy (recall/definition level)
- 5 medium (application)
- 3 hard ([EXAM]-level)

Ask one question at a time. Wait for my answer before showing the next.
After each answer: confirm right/wrong, brief explanation.
At the end: tell me my score and which concepts to re-study.
```

---

## 📋 Chapter Study Checklist

- [ ] Step 1: Got the overview before reading
- [ ] Step 2: Checked understanding after each concept
- [ ] Step 3: Connected all concepts to each other
- [ ] Step 4: Did exam-focused prep
- [ ] Step 5: Scored 7+/12 on self-test
- [ ] Added chapter to revision schedule

---

## ⏰ Compressed Version (When Short on Time)

If you only have 45 minutes:

1. **Step 1** (5 min) — reconnaissance prompt
2. **Step 2** condensed — one prompt covering all concepts at once
3. **Step 5** — 8-question quiz
