Physics in JEE: What You Need to Know

  • JEE Main: ~30% of paper (~25 questions of 90)
  • JEE Advanced: ~33% of paper — but highest difficulty
  • Strongest chapters: Mechanics, Electromagnetism, Optics

Chapter Sequence (Dependency Order)

Foundation:
  Kinematics → Laws of Motion → Work-Energy-Power

Build:
  SHM → Rotational Motion → Gravitation

Heat:
  Thermodynamics → Kinetic Theory

Electricity:
  Electrostatics → Current Electricity →
  Magnetic Effects → EMI → AC Circuits

Light:
  Ray Optics → Wave Optics

Modern:
  Dual Nature → Atoms & Nuclei → Semiconductors

Never jump ahead — each topic depends on previous ones.

Physics-Specific AI Prompts

Derivation Master:

"Derive [formula/result] from first principles for JEE.
 Show every step. Explain the physical meaning at each step.
 End with: when does this formula NOT apply?"

Dimensional Analysis:

"Verify the dimensional formula for [quantity].
 Show me how dimensional analysis can eliminate wrong
 answer options in JEE MCQs."

Graph Questions:

"JEE Physics is full of graph-based questions on [TOPIC].
 Describe 5 important graphs, what each axis represents,
 what the slope means, what the area under the curve means."

Numericals:

"Give me 5 numerical problems on [TOPIC] at JEE Main level.
 After I solve (just say 'solve it for me' if needed),
 show the systematic approach: given → find → formula → solve → check units."
