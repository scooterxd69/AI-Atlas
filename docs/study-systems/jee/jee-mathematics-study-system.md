## Mathematics in JEE

Maths is the differentiator at JEE Advanced. Most students who crack JEE Advanced do so because of strong Maths.

**Key insight:** JEE Maths tests *technique recognition*, not just calculation. Train yourself to identify the right technique in the first 30 seconds of reading a problem.

---

## Technique-First AI Approach

```
"I'm studying [CHAPTER] for JEE. Don't give me problems yet.
 First, list all the technique types that appear in JEE for this chapter.
 For each technique:
 - When to apply it (trigger conditions)
 - The core steps
 - One worked example"
```

This builds a mental catalogue of techniques before you drill problems.

## Chapter Sequence

```
Essential foundation:
  Quadratic Equations → Sequences & Series → Binomial Theorem

Geometry block:
  Straight Lines → Circles → Conics (Parabola → Ellipse → Hyperbola)

Trigonometry:
  Trig Functions → Inverse Trig

Calculus (largest chapter block):
  Limits → Continuity → Differentiability → Differentiation
  → Applications of Derivatives → Integration
  → Definite Integration → Differential Equations

Advanced algebra:
  Complex Numbers → Matrices & Determinants
  → P&C → Probability

3D block:
  Vectors → 3D Geometry
```

---

## Integration — Highest Priority Chapter

Integration is worth the most marks in JEE. Dedicate 3–4x more time to it.

```
"I'm studying Integration for JEE. Create a structured
 guide to all integration techniques in order of:
 1. Frequency in JEE
 2. Difficulty
 For each technique: definition, when to use, 2 worked examples."
```
