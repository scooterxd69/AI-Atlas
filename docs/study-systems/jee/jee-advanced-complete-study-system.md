## JEE Advanced vs JEE Main — Key Differences

| Aspect | JEE Main | JEE Advanced |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Question type | Mostly single-concept | Multi-concept, multi-step |
| Integer type | Some | Significant weightage |
| Multi-correct | No | Yes — partial marking |
| Difficulty | Moderate | High — often novel |
| Strategy | Accuracy + speed | Deep understanding |

**The AI difference:** For JEE Advanced, use AI to build *understanding*, not just solve problems. The exam tests whether you truly understand — you can't template your way through it.

---

## Advanced AI Workflow for JEE Advanced Chapters

```
For each chapter, run this sequence:

1. UNDERSTAND: "Explain [topic] from absolute first principles.
   I want to know WHY every formula is derived, not just HOW."

2. CONNECT: "What are all the JEE Advanced topics that connect
   to [topic]? Show me multi-concept problems that use this."

3. PREDICT: "What are the non-standard question types from
   [topic] that have appeared in JEE Advanced? What makes
   them tricky?"

4. VERIFY: "Ask me 5 questions that distinguish someone who
   truly understands [topic] vs someone who only memorised formulas."
```

## High-Priority Topics for JEE Advanced

### Physics
- Rotational Mechanics (highest weightage)
- Electrostatics + Capacitors
- Optics (especially Wave Optics)
- Modern Physics — Photoelectric, Nuclear
- Thermodynamics

### Chemistry
- Organic Chemistry — all mechanisms
- Coordination Compounds
- Electrochemistry
- P-Block elements (Groups 15, 16, 17)

### Mathematics
- Integration (single highest marks chapter)
- Complex Numbers
- P&C + Probability
- 3D Geometry + Vectors
