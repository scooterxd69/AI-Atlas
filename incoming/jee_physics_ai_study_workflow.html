1
Hint-first doubt solving
Always ask for the first hint, not the full answer. This forces your brain to do the remaining work — which is where learning actually happens.
Claude Gemini (free)
Try this prompt
"Don't solve this. Give me only the first physical insight I need to approach it: [paste question]. My attempt: [paste]. Where am I going wrong conceptually?"
2
Solution breakdown & generalisation
After solving, ask Claude to generalise — what's the template for this problem type? What variations does JEE ask?
Claude
Try this prompt
"I solved this problem [paste solution]. Now generalise it: what's the underlying template? Give me 3 JEE-level variants of this problem with increasing difficulty."
3
Photo-based doubt solver
Photograph a problem from HC Verma, DC Pandey, or your test paper. Upload directly to Claude for step-by-step breakdown.
Claude (image) Gemini (image)
Try this prompt
"[Upload photo of problem] Solve this step by step. After the solution, tell me: what concept is this really testing, and what's the 30-second mental shortcut for this type?"
4
Generate practice sets on demand
Ask Claude to generate 5 problems of a specific type, difficulty, or question format (integer, MCQ single/multiple, assertion-reason).
Claude ChatGPT (free)
Try this prompt
"Generate 5 JEE Advanced-level integer-type problems on Magnetic Effects of Current. Vary the scenario each time. Include answer keys and 2-line solution sketches."
Free tool by question type
Numerical / derivation
Claude, ChatGPT free, Gemini — all handle step-by-step maths
Graph-based / diagram MCQ
Upload image to Claude or Gemini — both read diagrams well
Assertion–Reason
Claude excels at explaining logical distinctions between assertions
Concept MCQ
Ask Claude to explain why each wrong option is wrong — not just the right answer
Never ask "solve this" for a problem you haven't attempted. Set a rule: minimum 5 minutes of solo effort before asking AI. The struggle is where synapses form.
